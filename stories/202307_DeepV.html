DeepVeeg

Deepfaking ecosystem response to climate extremes

Project summary

Forecasting ecosystem response to climate extremes critically depends on realistic models of the spatiotemporal behaviour of ecosystems in vegetation models, and of its dependence on environmental factors and macro-/microclimatic conditions. However, reliable scenario simulation and sensitivity analysis of ecosystem response in these vegetation models, i.e., predicting how ecosystem will respond to future climate extremes, remains limited due to the parametric simplicity of the current vegetation models and the lack of accurate data to constrain such models. Therefore, an innovation project is proposed to develop data-driven, spatially explicit ecosystem response models based on remote sensing data and conditional Generative Adversarial Networks (cGANs). cGAN’s are popular deep learning tools to generate deepfake data that correspond to realistic/actual data. cGAN’s have been successful in image and vision computing, video and language processing. Some examples, include cGAN generation of realistic fake true color and multispectral satellite images based on known environmental conditions, or projections of urban development based physics-constrained background conditions. Within the proposed DeepV project we will expand the use of cGANs with the development of techniques to generate fake but realistic satellite image time series of ecosystem response from globally-available Sentinel-2 remote-sensing data and environmental and hydrometeorological conditions (e.g. based on reanalysis data, topography, etc). The goal of the DeepV project therefore is to explore the use, expansion and development of cGANs with innovative techniques based on time series information and biophysical constraints. The developed cGANs will be trained on the Sentinel-2 satellite archives where both satellite, environmental and hydro-meteorological data are available to establish a climate-to-ecosystem response rule. Validation will consist of applying the techniques on historical Sentinel-2 time series of test sites not included in the model development, where the comparison between the cGAN predicted Sentinel-2 scenes and observed Sentinel-2 scenes will allow an independent quantitative validation of the developed methodologies. Once these cGANs have been created, optimised and validated, they can be utilised to simulate ecosystem response based on background environmental conditions and projected climate extremes. As such, they become tools for ecosystem sensitivity assessment. For example, by modifying the environmental and hydrometeorological conditions, one can simulate the expected ecosystem response over a prescribed landscape.

The selected PhD candidate will focus on using multi-source remote sensing in combination with snow models to establish links between local, regional, and large-scale processes to determine the origin of the variability of the surface mass balance. He/she will also participate in Mass2Ant field campaigns to the Belgian Princess Elisabeth station in east Antarctica to collect in-situ data on spatio-temporal variations in SMB.

Requierements

  • an MSc degree in remote sensing, meteorology, Earth system science, or a related field
  • ability to participate in (two) Antarctic field campaigns
  • excellent communication skills demonstrated through a well-written article or thesis, a public presentation or personal blog
  • written and spoken proficiency in the English language
  • experience with data analysis, programming and/or numerical modelling
