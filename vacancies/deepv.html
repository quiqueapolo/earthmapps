Post-doc vacancy

Deepfaking ecosystem response to climate extremes

Deepfaking ecosystem response to climate extremes

We are seeking a highly motivated and skilled postdoctoral researcher to join our interdisciplinary team for the DeepV project with the Division of Forest, Nature & Landscape within the KU Leuven. This project aims to develop innovative techniques using conditional Generative Adversarial Networks (cGANs) to model and forecast ecosystem response to climate extremes based on satellite data and environmental factors. The successful candidate will play a key role in expanding the use of cGANs and integrating time series information and biophysical constraints into the modeling framework.

Project summary

30-month postdoc project
KU Leuven
funded by the Belspo Stereo program

Project Description

The forecasting of ecosystem response to climate extremes is crucial for understanding and mitigating the impacts of climate change. However, existing vegetation models have limitations due to their parametric simplicity and the lack of accurate data to constrain them. The DeepV project seeks to overcome these limitations by harnessing the power of cGANs, which have shown promise in generating realistic synthetic data across various domains.

In this project, we will leverage the rich data available from the Sentinel-2 satellite archives, along with environmental and hydrometeorological data, to develop data-driven, spatially explicit models of ecosystem response. The primary objective is to create cGANs that can generate realistic satellite image time series depicting ecosystem responses to varying climate conditions. These models will be trained on historical data and validated using independent test sites, allowing for quantitative assessment of their predictive capabilities.

Responsibilities

  • Collaborate with the project team to design and implement cGAN-based models for ecosystem response simulation.
  • Integrate time series information and biophysical constraints into the cGAN framework.
  • Develop techniques to generate synthetic satellite image time series based on Sentinel-2 data and environmental conditions.
  • Validate and evaluate the performance of the developed methodologies using observed satellite image data.
  • Publish research findings in reputable scientific journals and present results at conferences.
  • Supervision of MSc theses student projects.
  • Active involvement in research team by means of (co-)supervision, outreach, idea generation etc.

Qualifications

  • A Ph.D. in a relevant field, such as environmental science, remote sensing, computer science, or a related discipline.
  • Strong background and experience in machine learning, deep learning, and image processing.
  • Proficiency in programming languages such as Python and experience with deep learning frameworks (e.g., TensorFlow, PyTorch).
  • Familiarity with remote sensing data, climate models, and environmental datasets.
  • Excellent analytical and problem-solving skills.
  • Demonstrated ability to work both independently and collaboratively in a research team.
  • Strong written and verbal communication skills in English.
